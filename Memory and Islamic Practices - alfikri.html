Memory and Islamic Practices

13 Dec
December 13, 2015

    If you really need to know and experience what I am talking about, I request you to do something along with me .. Believe me… This will really help you understand what I am going to talk about , Ready ?

Okay … I need you to remember 7 numbers … Don’t write it any where.. Just need to hold it in your mind.. Okay 23, 87,45,54, 63 ,112 ,9 …. Thank you very much .. Now just answer me for some questions.. What is 23 * 8 ? What you ate last morning? Where is your cell phone now?

You will have your answers… Thats all right… But What I really need is you to remember those 7 numbers in the exact order I told.. How many of you could remember all the seven exactly? Some 5 … Some 4 .. Some none.

Now I am going to explain you what you did While I told you those 7 numbers.. You stopped listening to all other sounds and things around you, Then brought your focus on me .. Then listened to those 7 numbers and holded it in your memory. Right?

You actually used two parts of your Brain for this process. Prefrontal Cortex and parietal lobe

With Parietal lobe which controls our sensory inputs, you blocked all other sounds and disturbances which enter your brain , Then with pre-frontal cortex you focused on my speech and memorized numbers.. But after you tried to answer a couple of questions, you almost lost what all those numbers are.. Right? Why is this is because you stored those memory in you temporary memory called Working Memory. Working Memory is temporary and very limited in capacity. Experiments proved that, Humans could hold a maximum of 7 items in working memory at a time. The average is 5. But how much number you recalled , when I asked? So there is some trouble ahhh??

No trouble ,it’s quite normal, When you hear something, you put that information into working memory , When you think about it, you push it to short term memory, When you repeat that information again and again, you commit that to long term memory

Okay Let me remind you some of the companions of Prophet Muhammed(S.A.W) who proved their extra-ordinary memory capacity narrating the most number of hadith.

The number one.. No doubt it’s Abu Hurairah (R.A). Narrated About 5374 Hadith

Second Abdulla ibn Abbas (R.A) – 2660

Third Aisha (R.A) – 2210. and so on…

How do they developed their extra-ordinary memory capacity? No-doubt, It’s the tawfeeq of Allah Subhanahuvathala, And by the blessings of Prophet (S.A). And how they got this tawfeeq?. No-doubt. It’s by just following the Deen of Allah(SWT). Which is complete in all senses and practical in all times and proved successful for every one who followed it.

The famous hadith scholar Zakariyya al-Khandlawi similarly relates in his book Hayat al-sahaba that Abu Hurayra said: “I recite istighfar (formula of asking forgiveness) 12,000 times daily” and that, according to his grandson, he had a piece of thread with 1,000 knots and would not go to sleep until he had said subhan allah (Glory to Allah) on all of these knots.

Is it their regular routine of practicing dikr boosted their capacity to memorize more information?

That’s what science had proven today, That practicing meditation(Dikr & Tasbeeh) could really stimulate the brain regions you had use a short while ago to memorize those seven numbers. The Pre-frontal Cortex and the Parietal lobe brain of our brain.

Just Imagine you are doing dikr now? What will you do? You will stop listening and thinking about all other things. And just focus your mind in the remembering Allah(SWT). And this activity will stimulate those two parts of our brain. By increasing the blood flow on those region and strengthening the neural connections in that area. Resulting in the increased ability to focus and learn even in the most disturbing situation.

This is the Deen of Allah (SWT). And today we say that the islamic way of life is old and not applicable in the current society. And we rush to train our children to prepare them to compete with the modern society. And we dont realize, that the western world is now only practicing meditation techniques to boost their mental and physical performance. Which is the same technique an unshcooled prophet prescribed For this ummath 1400 years ago ie, To practice Dikr 2 times a day with amost 12 hrs interval .ie, after fajr and asar. Ofcourse , the luckiest are the one who did it with faith and hope in the words of our prophet (SAW). There are thousands of examples like this.

We believe that geniuses are born. No!! Geniuses are made, with proper guidance and repeated practice.. And the best time to mold a genius is his childhood. And his best teachers are his parents. And his best way of life he could walk through is the Islamic Practices. So make sure ,you guide them through the right way all the time.
